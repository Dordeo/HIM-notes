# JOB Categories 
Retail Banking and Credit: Retail banker, credit analyst, relationship manager.

Investment and Corporate Finance: Financial analyst, trader, investment banker.

Risk and Compliance: Risk manager, actuary, compliance officer.

Emerging Technologies: Data scientist, blockchain developer, ESG analyst.


## Finance of the Future
#### The most common skills needed in the industry

Analytics, Financial modelling, critical thinking, attention to detail and communication. Adaptability and negotiation skills are also promoted.

# Jobs examples
### [[Retail Banker]]
[[Retail Banker]]s work directly with individual customers, offering services like savings and checking accounts, personal loans, mortgages, and financial advice. They typically operate in bank branches and help customers manage their day-to-day finances.

### [[Credit Analyst]]
[[Credit Analyst]]s assess the creditworthiness of individuals or businesses applying for loans. They analyze financial statements, credit reports, and market conditions to determine risk levels and recommend approval or denial of credit applications.

### [[Relationship Manager]]
[[Relationship Manager]]s build and maintain strong connections with clients, often in banking or financial services. Their goal is to understand client needs, offer tailored solutions, and ensure long-term satisfaction and loyalty.

### [[Financial Analyst]]
[[Financial Analyst]]s evaluate financial data to help businesses make informed decisions. They create forecasts, analyze trends, and assess investment opportunities, often working in corporate finance, investment firms, or government agencies.

### [[Trader]]
[[Trader]]s buy and sell financial instruments like stocks, bonds, commodities, or currencies. They aim to profit from market fluctuations and may work for investment banks, hedge funds, or independently.

### [[Investment Banker]]
[[Investment Banker]]s help companies raise capital by issuing stocks or bonds, and advise on mergers, acquisitions, and other financial transactions. They work in high-pressure environments and play a key role in corporate finance.

### [[Risk Manager]]
[[Risk Manager]]s identify, assess, and mitigate financial, operational, or strategic risks within an organization. They develop policies and strategies to minimize potential losses and ensure regulatory compliance.

### [[Actuary]]
[[Actuary]]s use mathematics, statistics, and financial theory to analyze risk and uncertainty, especially in insurance and pension sectors. They help design policies and predict future liabilities based on statistical models.

### [[Compliance Officer]]
[[Compliance Officer]]s ensure that a company adheres to legal and regulatory requirements. They monitor internal policies, conduct audits, and train staff to prevent violations and maintain ethical standards.

### [[Data Scientist]]
[[Data Scientist]]s extract insights from large datasets using statistical analysis, machine learning, and programming. They help organizations make data-driven decisions across industries like tech, finance, healthcare, and retail.

### [[Blockchain Developer]]
[[Blockchain Developer]]s design and implement decentralized applications and smart contracts using blockchain technology. They work on platforms like Ethereum or Bitcoin, focusing on security, transparency, and innovation in digital transactions.

### [[ESG Analyst]]
[[ESG Analyst]]s evaluate companies based on sustainability and ethical practices. They assess how businesses manage environmental impact, social responsibility, and corporate governance to guide investment decisions.
