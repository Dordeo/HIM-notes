# Five Pillars of personal Finance

## Earning

The process of **acquiring** money through work, investments or other income-generating activities such as salary or self employment.

Simply all money **earned**.
## Saving

Setting aside money for future use or financial goals, such as emergency funds a down payment or retirement.

All money **saved**.
## Spending
The act of exchanging money for goods, services or experience to satisfy personal needs or wants.

Money **spent** on **immediate return**.
## Investments
Money **spent** on the investments not the money **earned** from investments.
Investments include stocks, bonds and real estate.

Money **spent** on **return in the future**.
## Protecting
Money **spent** on safeguarding your wealth: insurance, estate planning, cybersecurity.


# Nominal Interest Rate vs Real Interest Rate

[[Interest Rate]] - additional charge you pay whenever you pay money back after taking a loan.

### Nominal Interest Rate
[[Nominal Interest Rate]] - The rate displayed by the bank or financial institution. Does not take inflation into account. 

### Real Interest Rate
[[Real Interest Rate]] - Interest Rate adjusted for inflation. Measures the true gain in purchasing power. Simplified formula: 
$$
\text{Real rate} ≈ \text{Nominal rate – Inflation}
$$

[[Inflation]] - process of increasing prices.
