# Lesson 8 – Promotion

## Marketing Mix
- Product – Meets customer needs, must be high quality.
- Price – Reflects value, competitive balance.
- Place – Right time/location (stores, online).
- Promotion – Communicate value.

## Promotion Tools
- Advertising – TV, print, digital, outdoor.
- Personal Selling – Face-to-face, trust.
- Sales Promotion – Discounts, samples.
- Public Relations – Reputation, credibility.

## Advertising Types
- Print – Tangible, targeted.
- Outdoor – Billboards, transit.
- Television – Audio-visual impact.
- Product Placement – Films, cultural relevance.
- Digital – Search, social, display.

## Case Studies
- Glossier – Social media growth.
- Tupperware – Home parties success.
- Avon – Hybrid social selling.
- Always – “Run Like A Girl” empowerment.
- Gillette – “Best Men Can Be” cultural debate.

## Endorsements & Influencers
- Celebrity Endorsement – Visibility, credibility.
- Influencer Marketing – Authentic partnerships.

## Messaging
- Taglines / Slogans – Stickiness, emotion, differentiation.
- Examples: [[Taglines]], [[Slogans]]

## Public Relations
- Crisis management, earned media.
- Trade Shows – B2B growth.
- Networking – Trust, referrals.
- Earned Media – 3–5x more credible.

## Key Points
- Purpose-driven campaigns matter.
- Public Relations builds credibility.
- B2B promotion thrives on Trade Shows + Networking.
- Authentic storytelling strengthens brand connections.
