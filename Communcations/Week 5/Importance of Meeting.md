# Meetings
Are very important. Poorly run meetings are wasting on average 31 hours a month, which translates into 4 working day. Meeting can also influence **employee morale, psychological wellbeing** and effectiveness. 

## Meeting Structure

#### Before a Meeting
Prepare Agendas.

#### During a Meeting
Use Agendas.

#### After a group meeting
Minutes to discuss. 


### What is Agenda
[[Agenda]] - expected topics that are going to be covered. Think of it as a checklist of things you need to do.
#### Agenda Structure

##### Specific
Name of the task that need to be done

##### Results Oriented
Description, minimal, of what needs to be done.

##### Timed
Time of how long the meeting can be.

##### Realistic
Realistic goals.

##### Accountability
Who does what.

#### How to set an Agenda
###### Assign Roles
Chair or facilitator

Time keeper

## Why meetings can be weakened
- no planning / agenda
- not following the plan/ agenda
- Not having a record of what you are about to do
- No follow up on the decision made

## How meetings can be prepared
#### Prepare Purpose
Define a clear objective that you want to address during the meeting. Do not put vague meeting title like: "Daily Meeting".

#### Participants
Select the participations that are essential for the meeting, not all participants that are eligible.

#### Process
Set and follow the agenda.

#### Pay Off
Clarify the outcomes that need to be achieved by the end of the meeting.

#### Preparation
Share the meeting slides with the group.

## Listening to the lecture
- Body language
- Key Words
- Correlation between what they said and what they showed on the slides
- Intonation 

## Evaluate Listening Strategies 
- **Body language** is effective because it can help understand approach and emotions
- **Key Words** - Vocabulary - it paints a bigger picture about the lecturers perception, if you understand the key words you are better suited to the topic.
- There was a positive **correlation between the slides and what they talked about**, there was a picture of them and not just AI gen content. However sometime the slides did not follow wont the lecturers were talking about.
- **Intonation** - a big factor that explains the lecturers emotions about a certain situation for example if the situation is more personal or more distant.

## 3 most effective strategies
Body language
Key Words
Intonation
