# Customer Promise — Summary

## Customer Expectations
- [[Customer Expectations]] are shaped by **advertising**, **word of mouth**, **social media**, **past experiences**.
- Customers compare each experience to their **best recent experience**, not just competitors.

## Overpromise & Under-deliver
- Examples: Fyre Festival, Samsung Galaxy Fold, Volkswagen, Google Glass, Apple AirPods Max, Tidal.
- Lesson: Overpromising damages **Trust** and is very difficult to recover.

## Underpromise & Over-deliver
- Examples: Ritz-Carlton, Patagonia, Disney, Amazon Prime, IKEA.
- Lesson: Realistic expectations + exceeding them builds **Loyalty** and strengthens reputation.

## Customer Promise
- [[Customer Promise]] is what a company promises to deliver — the consistent [[Customer Experience]] customers should expect.
- Distinct from **tagline**, **vision**, **mission**, **values**.
- Holds the company accountable for consistent [[CX]].

## Customer Promise Model
Seven steps to create a strong promise:
1. Understand needs → via [[Qualitive Research]] & [[Quantitative Research]].
2. Define experience → clearly state what customers should feel.
3. Differentiate → show how you stand apart from competitors.
4. Collaborate → involve multiple departments and external experts.
5. Clarity → use clear, jargon-free language.
6. Shortlist → test options internally and externally.
7. Decide → choose a simple, relatable, memorable promise.

## Benefits
- Provides **focus** & **clarity**.
- Improves understanding of customer needs.
- Differentiates from competitors.
- Ensures **consistency** in delivery.
- Inspires & motivates teams.
- Supports **growth** and reduces **churn**.

---

✅ Takeaway: A strong [[Customer Promise]] aligns expectations with delivery, avoids [[Overpromising]], and creates [[Trust]], [[Loyalty]], and [[Differentiation]] in the market.
