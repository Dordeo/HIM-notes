# Understanding & Defining Customer Experience (CX)

## 1. How does CX differ from Customer Service?

- CX is emotional and holistic, covering every interaction
- Customer Service is functional and task-based

## 2. Define Customer Experience.

- The emotional response a customer has from every interaction with a company

## 3. Define Customer Service.

- The functional support provided before, during, and after a purchase

## 4. What are the emotional components of CX?

- Feelings such as satisfaction, trust, and loyalty generated from interactions

## 5. Why is trust important in Customer Experience?

- Trust influences customer loyalty and their willingness to recommend a company

## 6. Explain the concept of the ‚ÄúEmotional Piggybank.‚Äù

- Positive experiences deposit emotional credit
- Failures withdraw credit
- Consistent excellence builds a buffer of goodwill

## 7. What are some ways companies can build trust with customers?

- Deliver on promises
- Exceed expectations
- Resolve issues effectively
- Provide authentic experiences

## 8. Why do customers no longer trust companies as they once did?

- Overexposure to advertising and unmet promises
- Preference for peer recommendations
